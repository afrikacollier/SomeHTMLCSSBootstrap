Music


Posted by Afrika Collier at Thursday, October 24, 2019 11:56:55 PM
I usually don’t really listen to music but when I do I prefer it silent. I do appreciate the music
that gives the feel of community. Whenever there are people jumping, swinging their hips, or stomping
their feet it gives off a feeling of community. Sometimes I even want to join in the dancing. The music
that makes you want to dance regardless of whether you can or not. The music that you hear out on the
streets. Sometimes maybe it's a little humming noise that I heard walking downtown. Sometimes it’s not
even necessarily the words but the soul put into the sound of the beat and rhythm. Then it can also be
that wholesome sound in church. Where some long notes are stretched out on an organ. There’ s even
traditional dances danced to traditional music. One friend from Georgia (not the state) was showing me
a YouTube video of a traditional folk dance that people did throughout the city of Georgia. There’s even
a periodical made by some folk in California with nothing else to do called “Folk Dance Scene” listing
different folk dances around the country. They even teach people how to dance at their group meetings.
I suppose dancing wouldn’t come as something I enjoy watching and doing sometimes if it wasn’t for the
joy of music. This music bringing about community. Music is always something that’s shared. You don’t go
into a store and ask for earphones and they only give you one earbud. Earphones always come with two
earbuds whether you like it or not. However, I guess music can also be for yourself. Sometimes I hear
my mom making up her own lyrics “just because”. Other times, my mom and I would be on a road trip
listening to music together and then start making are own lyrics about anything and everything. Music
is meant to bring about community. In the church growing up there was praise dance. Our
long-outstretched arms were used to convey a meaning a long with our large dramatic performance.
Whether that meaning was actually any meaning at all was up to how the music led you to feel in that
moment. I always really enjoyed this. Or the times at the Kroc center where the kids would beat on
drums to make their own meanings of the music was enjoyable when they beat the drums with rhythm but
of course five year olds have their own since of rhythm.
