Dew Drops


Posted by Afrika Collier at Thursday, September 5, 2019 10:55:08 PM
A hint of grey and black and white make up the subtle tone of the picture
“Drops of Dew”. Water paints onto the canvas (the leaf) like sprinkles on ice cream,
thus, creating an illusory image and mystery behind perhaps the many thoughts
that can be derived. The image is mysterious but simple. It does it’s due as nature
allows rain to drop from the clouds to sprinkle on the ice cream. Later the sprinkles
evaporate and return to the sky to repeat their cycle. Veins of life are spread across
the smooth surface of the leaf. Reminding me of how the blood courses through the body
to bring about our own life. The large drops and the small drops, the drops that both
smear and dampen can all be found on the leaf to tear or heal, I do not know. I do know
the sun rises and the stars fall. The larger the drop the faster they fall while the
smaller the drop the more they’ll rise one day. The cycle repeats and the smaller drops
will continue to be the nourishment for the leaf. While the larger drops that fall will
be replaced. Capitalism always runs that way. Unlike sprinkles— whose color may differ
but size remain relatively the same— dew drops may have large and small drops. Perhaps,
they represent they represent the rich and the poor, but in the end, does it matter anymore.
They all go back to the same cycle: to rinse, to survive, to repeat, and to hopefully
live out the rest of their lives.
The small may make up the larger of the dew drops, while the large make up the smaller.
However, the essence of the larger (money) make up the majority while the smaller continue
to have the same fate. The smaller repeat the cycle. Some may combine and become conglomerates
but others may have the same fate as some other Big Companies and fall all the same off the
leaf. Sometimes I wonder why there are not more leaves. Can leaves change? Can one dew drop hop
on to a non-existing leaf? Or is there one of government for the people and this government for all?
However, dew drops are different, leaves are different but there is only one leaf. Can the leaf
die? Can it wither and wilt? Will it cease to exist? Can a stranger walk by and step on it?
What happens then?
After all, this leaf does not have a name. It’s not stamped as democracy, republic, communism,
autocracy, or fascism. Regardless of the unknown the leaf remains the same. The picture will
keep its dismal look will never change. The photographer will stay behind the lens and pretend
he knows what he thought when he took the picture hoping that other people will understand.
The photographer could also think there are multiple interpretations and not a single one is
correct or wrong. What’s good for one may not be good for the other. The dismal look, the may
drops as sprinkles or sprinkles as drops may never really be understood. For there are different
colors, different classes (the rich and the poor), and different governments in high time these all
give rise to different cycles.
