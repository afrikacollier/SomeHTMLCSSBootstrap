Greatest Fear


Posted by Afrika Collier at Thursday, October 10, 2019 11:56:16 PM
My greatest fear after college is not being able to find a job that I want. I originally
wanted to do app development but everything seems so competitive lately. Everybody says
that you need more and more education as the years go by in order to get the position that
you want. In the past there used to be apprenticeships and you learned from a master or
something. However, nowadays you need a degree then another degree to prove that your
invaluable for the position. There are still internships available but the internships
seem to be getting more and more competitive. It makes me wonder whether there are enough
people filling the positions since there’s such a thing as people being underqualified to
do the different positions, and therefore, leaves vacancy. However, is there enough room for
qualified people to teach what the world deems underqualified people? Does school ever really
teach you everything you need to know? I think there is always room for improvement. What if
someone is book smart but not necessarily paper smart? This can also be the case for many
people. Some people just suck at taking tests and it is not exactly a good representation
of their ability. I know for one thing, one trig/pre-cal exam sophomore year of high school
I got 100% on half of the test. I only managed to attempt half of the test. Working a job
seems different than actually being in school. These instances I felt in the library. If I
have a question that I do not know, I just ask someone who does. Same thing if someone has
a question they do not know, they just ask me or whoever else knows.
Though I do have other fears such as not eating a meal everyday -- starving because I couldn’t even get
a job at McDonalds. Not being able to afford a car or a livelihood. What if one day I wind up on the
side of the road with no food, water, or clothes on my back. This all ties into not being able to find
a job in the end. What if the apartment I live in turns out to be a hole that I’ll never be able to
crawl out? There could be water dripping from the ceilings fall on my bed. I might have to move the
bed but there are more water drippings from the spot I moved the bed too. Then I have to put buckets
on the floor and buckets on top of the bed. Then ask the landlord whether they can fix the ceiling
but the landlord pretends there busy and no one comes to help after several months. Then my parents
find me, after several years of not communicating because I don’t want to tell them all my dreams
and aspirations and their expectations have all went down the drain of the garbage disposal. That
is what I’m afraid of becoming the garbage disposal.
